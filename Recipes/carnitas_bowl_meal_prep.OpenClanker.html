Carnitas Bowl (Pressure Cooker)

Components: Pressure-cooked pork carnitas + Mexican rice + seasoned beans + sautéed bell peppers

Storage: Keeps 4–5 days refrigerated (pork freezes well)

1) Pressure Cooker Carnitas (Pork Shoulder)

Ingredients (6–8 servings)

  • 3–4 lb pork shoulder, cut into large chunks
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 2 tsp cumin
  • 2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1–2 tsp chili powder
  • 1 cinnamon stick, broken into peices
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 1 orange (juice + spent halves)
  • 1 lime (juice)
  • 1 cup chicken stock

Method

  1. Toss pork with salt and spices.
  2. Add pork, garlic, onion, cinnamon, bay, citrus juice + halves, and stock to pressure cooker.
  3. Cook on high pressure 45–55 minutes. Natural release 15 minutes, then vent.
  4. Remove pork and shred. Discard solids.
  5. Reduce cooking liquid using sauté mode until slightly thick.
  6. Toss shredded pork with reduced liquid.
  7. Optional: Broil 5–8 minutes for crispy edges.
Store some reduced liquid separately and spoon over when reheating to prevent dryness.

2) Mexican Rice

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 3 1/2–4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt to taste

Method

  1. Rinse rice thoroughly.
  2. Toast rice in oil until lightly golden.
  3. Add onion and garlic; cook briefly.
  4. Stir in tomato sauce, broth, cumin, and salt.
  5. Simmer covered 15–18 minutes. Rest 10 minutes, then fluff.
Let cool uncovered before portioning to avoid condensation.

3) Seasoned Beans (Black or Pinto)

Ingredients

  • 2 ~16oz cans beans w/ liquid(or ~3 cups cooked)
  • 1 ~16oz can diced tomatos
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 serrano or jalapeno, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Mexican oregano
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Splash of stock or water

Method

  1. Sauté onions over medium-high heat until soft
  2. Add garlic and spicy-pepper
  3. Add tomatoes and simmer for until slightly reduced
  4. Add beans, spices, and liquid.
  5. Simmer 5–10 minutes until slightly thickened.
  6. Adjust salt and add lime juice if desired.

4) Sautéed Bell Peppers

Ingredients

  • 3 bell peppers (mixed colors), sliced
  • 1/2 red onion (optional)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt
  • Lime juice (to finish)

Method

  1. Heat oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Cook peppers (and onion) 5–7 minutes until softened but slightly crisp.
  3. Add spices and salt; cook 1 minute more.
  4. Finish with lime juice.
Slightly undercook for better texture when reheating.

Assembly (Per Bowl)

  • 1 cup rice
  • 3–5 oz carnitas
  • 1/2 cup beans
  • 1/2 cup sautéed peppers

Optional Add-Ons (Store Separately)

  • Pickled red onions
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Cotija or queso fresco
  • Salsa or verde sauce
  • Avocado (add day-of)

Storage Strategy

  • Keep components separate for best texture.
  • Freeze half the carnitas if prepping for more than 4 days.
  • Reheat pork and rice with reserved cooking liquid.
  • Add cold toppings after reheating.
