Easy Low-Calorie High-Protein Alfredo Pasta

Adapted from a recipe by Joshua Weissman.

Macros (per serving)

  • Calories: ~470
  • Protein: ~52 g
  • Carbohydrates: ~53 g
  • Fat: ~6 g

Ingredients

  • 1 head cauliflower (~2 lb / 900 g), cut into florets
  • Kosher salt and black pepper
  • 1.5 lb (680 g) boneless skinless chicken breasts, sliced into thin cutlets
  • 1/2 lb (225 g) dried fettuccine
  • Cooking spray
  • 1/2 cup skim milk
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmigiano Reggiano
  • 1/4 cup grated Pecorino Romano
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 tsp chicken bouillon powder

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add cauliflower florets and cook until very soft (about 10–12 minutes).
  2. Season chicken with salt and pepper and pat dry. Cook in a lightly sprayed skillet over medium-high heat, about 2–3 minutes per side, until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). Let rest and slice into thin strips.
  3. Transfer cooked cauliflower to a blender. Add half the milk, the cheeses, garlic, bouillon powder, and a little salt. Blend until smooth. Add the remaining milk as needed to achieve a creamy Alfredo-like consistency.
  4. Cook the pasta in salted water according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water.
  5. Add the cooked pasta to a skillet with the sauce and toss to coat. Add small amounts of pasta water as needed to loosen the sauce and help it emulsify.
  6. Divide pasta among four bowls and top with sliced chicken. Add extra grated parmesan if desired.

Notes

  • Additional seasonings can be added to the chicken, though they may change the macro values.
  • If a blender is unavailable, a food processor or immersion blender can also puree the sauce.
